What you’ll find here

Slow North Living is a beginner - friendly space for learning how to care for houseplants in real life, not perfect conditions.

The focus is on low - maintenance plants (my favorite kind), simple seasonal care routines, and small habits that actually work. There’s no pressure to get everything right - just thoughtful choices, patience, and learning together while enjoying the process.

Here you’ll find:

  • Easy houseplants for beginners.
  • Low - maintenance indoor plant care tips.
  • Plants that tolerate low light and dry indoor air.
  • Guidance for apartment living and small spaces.

A realistic approach to indoor plants

I’m not a plant professional - just someone learning as I go. After recently moving to Canada, I’ve had to adjust not only to a new place but also to a very different climate and longer winters. I’m figuring out how to care for plants in this environment through trial, error, and patience, and I share my experience so your plant journey can feel a little simpler and more approachable.

Plant care doesn’t have to be perfect. Choosing the right plant and understanding the season is often enough. And growth looks different in every home, so be gentle with yourself.

Recommendations are based on my own experiance, thorough research and everyday conditions — missed waterings, changing light, and dry winter air.

Growing plants in northern climates

Indoor plant care can feel a little different in northern regions. Between long winters, changing light throughout the year, and dry indoor air from heating, plants don’t always grow the way we expect them to. After moving to a new climate myself, I had to adjust my approach and let go of the idea that plant care looks the same everywhere.

Slow North Living focuses on finding the right plants for these conditions - and creating simple solutions at home to help them thrive. No complicated routines, just adaptable houseplants and small adjustments that make plant care easier throughout the year.
